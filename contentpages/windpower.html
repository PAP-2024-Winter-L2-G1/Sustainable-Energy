About Wind Power

What is Wind Power?

Wind power is a renewable energy source harnessed from the kinetic energy of the wind to generate electricity. It relies on the use of wind turbines, which are large structures equipped with blades that rotate when exposed to the force of the wind. As the blades turn, they drive a generator, converting the rotational energy into electrical power. Wind power has gained widespread recognition as an environmentally friendly alternative to traditional fossil fuels, as it produces electricity without emitting greenhouse gases or other harmful pollutants.
